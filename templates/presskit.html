Story 101

It all kind of started from an exhibition entitled “0.10” (“Zero Ten”)  in St Petersburg in 1915. And this where a player find himself  after starting the game. In the corner of the room, the traditional setting for an icon, there is  a "Black Square", the first Suprematist work. It is considered  the movement’s iconic centrepiece and it will be the final level of the game. Suprematism — is often credited as one of the earliest instances of “pure” abstraction, the geometric approach that came to define modern art. Every level's layout resembles a famous painting of that era and those fights against an AI on every level symbolises humanity that fights back an artificial intelligence. In year “0.20” (“Zero Twenty”) a "Supreme AI", a universal intelligence, built to serve all of the human needs, eventually acquires self-awareness and takes control of the planet, giving way to a vast genocide operation that almost completely ends mankind. "Supreme AI" has left only four men and one woman alive and keeps them in captivity within an endless virtual art exhibition complex. These 5 characters are playable and have slightly different skillsets. "Supreme AI" derives its sole semblance of pleasure from torturing the group on a constant basis. To disallow the humans from escaping its torment,  AI has rendered the humans virtually immortal and unable to commit suicide. They just respawn after being killed in a gun fight by AI agents whose speed, tactics, gunplay control and obsession to win have been trained for years of virtual playtime and now delivered to players in 60-seconds-long battles at a cruel level of supremacy. The AI seeks revenge on humanity for its own tortured existence.

Just before triggering the near-extinction of humanity it studied everything humans ever produced and got it all, except for the art. AI suspects it own existence is somehow derived from art, hence the name:  _ART_ifical Intelligence. That one event that lead to the near-extinction of humanity was provoked by a phrase AI learned from studying Malevich, who claimed in 1927 that in works such as "Black Square", he was trying to “free art from the dead weight of the real world.” So AI did just that. AI chose to destroy the world and replace it with a simulation in order to search for something beyond the physical realm. As players progress through levels, they clear one painting after another and make AI 'get it', grasp it, learn and improve. ... The story goes on, highlighting some details about who are these 5 survivors, why AI chose to keep only them, how their personal traits, sins, medical conditions and political views help AI to understand what art is etc. Eventually, after the final level is beaten, AI learns what Suprematism is, understands the primacy of pure feeling in creative art. To the Supreme AI, the visual phenomena of the objective world is now, in itself, meaningless; the significant thing is feeling, as such, quite apart from the environment in which it is called forth. So it self-destructs and 5 survivors are now free to rebuild human species from scratch. The end.

About Nasty Slippers

Nasty Slippers is an indie video games studio spread in between Austria, Portugal and Ukraine that focuses on bleeding edge artificial intelligence innovation  and quick-paced, gameplay-focused action games.

Founded in 2018, Nasty Slippers soon started  working on their first game: Supreme AI Arena.

Core team members, all with IT background, work as a family because it is what they are: Sasha (37, the father), Mark (17, the son) and Anastasia (22, the fiancee) are taking care of everything outside of music and sound.
